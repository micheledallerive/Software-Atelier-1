Turing Award 1993

Winners:
Juris Hartmanis and
Richard Edwin Stearns

Juris Hartmanis and Richard Edwin Stearns won the Turing Award in 1993 by recognition of a paper published in 1965. In 1960 Stearns joined Juris Hartmanis at the General Electric Research Laboratory where they started working together. They had initially worked on the structural decomposition of sequential machines into smaller sequential machines that accomplish the same tasks, then they moved to the topic of computational complexity. The Paper “On the Computational Complexity of Algorithms” defines a concept of complexity of an Algorithm, defines the concept of a complexity class and their correlation with an infinite sequence of increasingly hard problems.

The complexity class itself is defined as a set of problems solvable in a specified time bound:

  • A small change in the time bound enables variable ratio changes.
  • For example, fastest algorithm takes a time proportional to n, n2 , n3 ,2n , ecc...

Complexity measure defined by computation time on Turing machines helped the establishment of complexity theory as a fundamental part of the discipline.
