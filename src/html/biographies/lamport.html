Leslie Lamport

Biography

Leslie B. Lamport, born Febraury 7, 1941, New York, New York, is an American computer scientist. He is best recognized for his seminal work in distributed systems and as the creator and author of the original handbook for the document preparation system LaTeX. Lamport won the Turing Award in 2013 for fundamental contributions to the theory and practice of distributed and concurrent systems, notably the invention of concepts such as causality and logical clocks, safety and liveness, replicated state machines, and sequential consistency. Lamport got the award during his time at Microsoft, making him the fifth member of the Microsoft Research team to receive the honor.

Education and carrer

Lamport attended the Bronx High School of Science in New York in the late 1950s before graduating from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) with a bachelor's degree in mathematics in 1960. He continued his undergraduate studies at Brandeis University in Waltham, Massachusetts, where he earned a master's degree in 1963 and a doctorate in 1972.

Lamport taught mathematics at Marlboro College in Marlboro, Vermont, from 1965 until 1969. He worked as a computer scientist for Massachusetts Computer Associates from 1970 until 1977. He, then, he served at SRI International in Menlo Park, California, from 1977 to 1985 and from 1985 to 2001, he worked at Digital Equipment Corporation's Systems Research Center in Palo Alto, California. In 2001, Lamport accepted a post as lead computer scientist at Microsoft Research in Mountain View, California.
