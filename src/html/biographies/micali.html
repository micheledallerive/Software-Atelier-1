Silvio Micali

Biography

Silvio Micali, born October 13, 1954, in Palermo, Sicily, is an Italian computer scientist. Micali got a bachelor's degree in mathematics from Rome's La Sapienza University in 1978 and a doctorate in computer science from the University of California, Berkeley in 1982, for work supervised by Manuel Blum. Since 1983, Micali has been a member of the Electrical Engineering and Computer Science Department at MIT.

Cryptography, zero knowledge, pseudorandom generation, secure protocols, and mechanism design are some of his research interests. Micali is well known for his early work on public-key cryptosystems, pseudorandom functions, digital signatures, oblivious transfer, and secure multiparty computation, as well as being a co-inventor of zero-knowledge proofs. In 2012, he got the Turing Award along with Shafi Goldwasser, for transformative work that laid the complexity-theoretic foundations for the science of cryptography, and in the process pioneered new methods for efficient verification of mathematical proofs in complexity theory. He founded Alogrand in 2017.
