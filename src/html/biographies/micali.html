Silvio Micali

Biography

Silvio Micali, born October 13, 1954, in Palermo, Sicily, is an Italian computer scientist. Micali got a bachelor's degree in mathematics from Rome's La Sapienza University in 1978 and a doctorate in computer science from the University of California, Berkeley in 1982, for work supervised by Manuel Blum. Micali has been a member of the MIT Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science since 1983.

Cryptography, zero knowledge, pseudorandom generation, secure protocols, and mechanism design are some of his research interests. Micali is well known for his early work on public-key cryptosystems, pseudorandom functions, digital signatures, oblivious transfer, and secure multiparty computation, as well as being a co-inventor of zero-knowledge proofs. He and Shafi Goldwasser shared the Turing Award in 2012 for groundbreaking work that established the complexity-theoretic foundations for the science of cryptography while also pioneering new methods for efficient verification of mathematical proofs in complexity theory. He founded a blockchain-based cryptocurrency platform called Alogrand in 2017.
